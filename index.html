About

Engineering depth with research-driven curiosity

This portfolio keeps the original core story and expands it with the academic, technical, and professional depth reflected in Ashim’s resume — presenting a more complete picture of an engineer working across structural systems, seismic behavior, finite element analysis, and emerging data-driven methods for infrastructure intelligence.

Ashim Panthi is an Engineer-in-Training (EIT) with a Bachelor’s degree in Civil Engineering from Tribhuvan University and is currently pursuing a Master of Science in Structural Engineering at Iowa State University. His work lives at the intersection of structural design, seismic performance, computational analysis, and structural health monitoring.

He brings hands-on experience in analysis and design of steel and reinforced concrete systems for buildings and infrastructure, including cargo facilities, educational buildings, earthquake-resistant earthen structures, and vibration-conscious steel systems in dense urban environments. Alongside practice, he develops Python- and MATLAB-based engineering workflows for feature extraction, machine learning, and data interpretation from structural response signals.

Ashim is especially interested in structural consulting roles that involve modeling, design calculations, construction documentation, code-based decision making, and innovative methods that make infrastructure safer, smarter, and more resilient in high-risk environments.
